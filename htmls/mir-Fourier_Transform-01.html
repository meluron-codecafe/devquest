Sinusoid

A sinusoid is a function given by $y = A\sin(\omega t + \phi)$
where,
$\omega = 2\pi f$: Angular frequency (radians)
$\phi$: Phase (radians)

$f$ is basically the number of cycles a sinusoid completes in 1 sec.

Sinusoids at different frequency

Frequency/angular frequency represents how faster a sinusoid is going to complete its full cycle. A sinusoid with $f = 1 hz$ or $\omega = 2\pi f$ implies that the sinusoid completes it cycle in $1 sec$.

Sinusoids with different phase

Think of phase as a metric that traslate the sinusoid along the time axis. Adding $+ve$ phase, sinusoids moves backward and adding $-ve$ phase moved the sinusoid forward.
