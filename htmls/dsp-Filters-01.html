Filters

We will answer these quesions:

  1. What are filters?
  2. How to build one?
  3. What has convolution operation to do with filters?

A filter $f$ is a function that takes an input signal and gives an ouput signal.

$f: x[n] \to y[n]$

In general filter means to get rid of some of the components from a mix. Think of filters as special functions that are able to separate these components from a mix.

A digital signal is an array of numbers so what does it even mean to filter them? Are they made of some components that we can filter?

According to Fourier, any signal can be broken into weighted sum of sinusoids at different frequencies. So, filtering can mean to get rid of some of these components. But now the question is how can we do that?

Experiment¶

Let say I have a signal that is a mix of two sinusoids at freq $f_1$ and $f_2$. Now my task is to filter out $f_1$ component from the signal. Let's make an attempt to do this.

Now the task is to remove say $f_1=15 hz$ component from the signal. A naive way for this simple problem is to create a reference sinusoid at $f_1$ and subtract it.

In [31]:
ref = np.sin(2*np.pi*f1*mix_t)
In [42]:
display(ms.plot1d(

display(ms.plot1d(

    xlabel="Time (sec)",

display(ms.plot1d(
    (mix_y - ref, mix_t),
    title="$Signal - Reference$",
    xlabel="Time (sec)",
    ylabel="Displacement",
    ylim=(-2, 2),
))

So we observe that the idea worked for region 1 and 3 but we ended up adding 15 hz component (phase reversed) to region 2.
